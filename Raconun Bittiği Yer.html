Where the Code Ends: Dishonor in the Guise of Mafia, Murder for the Sake of “Influence” September 01, 2025 Where the Code Ends: Dishonor in the Guise of Mafia, Murder for the Sake of “Influence” Author: Melike Balkın Karakaya | September 01, 2025 There was once a rule—even in the underworld: “Weapons are not spoken of in the presence of women and children.” What we witness today shows that even this code has been discarded. Lives are not the only targets anymore; the spouses and children left behind are also dragged into the crosshairs. Worse still, this brutality is displayed on social media as a badge of honor in the pursuit of becoming an “influencer.” This is not merely a murder; this is the showcase of social decay. 1. Unemployed Youth and the Fake “Mafia” Culture Many young people, unemployed and stripped of hope for the future, are drawn into a counterfeit mafia culture under the dream of going to Europe. They possess neither ideals nor human values. What is sold to them as “manhood” or “power” is, in truth, dishonor and crime. For the sake of a few street videos, they do not hesitate to commit murder. 2. Social Media and the Exhibition of Violence Today’s notion of “influence” has turned violence into content. A generation that once looked up to singers, writers, and scientists now takes as role models criminals brandishing guns on TikTok. Killers believe that sharing footage of their crimes and becoming “trending” is an achievement. This is not merely individual deviation; it is the moment a generation’s tether snaps. 3. Erosion of Trust in the Justice System Turkey’s justice system has reached a point where a large portion of society lives with the feeling that “justice is not being served.” Crimes that go unpunished, performative trials, and a legal order perceived to work “depending on who you are” push people toward taking justice into their own hands. If this course is not reversed, we will be dragged into a society where everyone enforces their own law and chaos becomes the rule. 4. A Scientific Reading of Society The collapse of a society does not begin with the economy or politics; it begins at the point where the safety of women and children disappears. This is the most visible sign of moral ground loss. Today, unemployed young men acting as hitmen under the guise of mafia culture is, in reality, the normalization of lawlessness. If we remain silent today, tomorrow—in the world our own children inhabit—conscience will be the exception, and violence the norm. Note: This article does not target individuals; it is a social-scientific commentary that analyzes societal dynamics through a current event.
