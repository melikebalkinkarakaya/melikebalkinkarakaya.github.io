The Mindset That Sees Shopping Mall Queues as Prosperity

In recent years, Turkey has been experiencing one of the deepest economic fractures in its history. During a period in which purchasing power has eroded, prices have soared, and the middle class has collapsed, social behavior has also visibly changed.

Yet despite this reality, there are still people on social media who can say: “There’s a queue at the shopping mall, so the economy must be doing well. No one is hungry.”

The shock created by this statement does not stem from the economy being bad, but from the collapse of society’s capacity to interpret the economy. Shopping mall queues are not indicators of economic prosperity; they are sociological, psychological, and behavioral outcomes of economic collapse.

We Understand the Economy Through Data, Not Images

The logic of “There’s a queue → the economy is good” has no intersection whatsoever with scientific thinking. Because the economy is not an image—it is a set of indicators.

So What Does the Picture Look Like in Turkey?

Minimum Wage & Exchange Rates

In 2025, the net minimum wage is 22,104 TL. While it was approximately 630 USD when announced, today’s rate brings it down to around 520 USD. The Euro has reached 49–50 TL, an 8.5 to 9-fold increase compared to 2019.

Food Inflation (2019 vs 2024)

A basket of 20 basic food items cost 266 TL in 2019. By 2024, the same basket cost 2,804 TL—a 952% increase.

With these data points clearly in front of us, interpreting shopping mall crowds as a “sign of prosperity” is like explaining an X-ray using coffee fortune-telling. This is not merely a lack of information; it is a lack of data literacy.

Conclusion: Economic Reality Is Not Undermined by Facts—Nor Saved by Hostility Toward Atatürk.
